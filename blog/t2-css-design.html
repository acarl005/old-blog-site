Padding, Borders, and Margins

Feb 11, 2015

Ever wonder how stuff is positioned on a webpage? So do I. It's done with padding, borders, and margins. Imagine the webpage as a party of knights, where each knight is a picture, paragraph, or piece of web content! Each knight has a suit of armor, like the borders on a website. These help make things look nice and organized. But inside these borders is padding, space separating the borders from the content. This is like the clothes beneath the knight's armor!

Visualizing this model.

Finally, imagine they have a force field around them that keeps walls and other knights at a distance. These are the margins. The margins are the outermost separation, pushing away from the rest of the elements. The content and the border are the only parts that are actually visible though. Together, these map out a webpage to make it look polished and pretty!
